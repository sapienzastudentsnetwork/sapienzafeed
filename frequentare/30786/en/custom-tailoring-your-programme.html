WHAT IS A TRAINING PATHWAY?
A training pathway defines the courses to be examined to obtain the qualification
degree (three-year degree). Students need an educational path to book or take examinations of their choice, but only those that are compulsory for all students.
Only those exams that are compulsory for all.


WHEN TO SUBMIT ONE'S EDUCATIONAL PATHWAY?
At present, the only non-compulsory subjects in the three-year degree course in Computer Science are allocated to the third
year.

Therefore, students are invited to present their educational path before the beginning of the exam session of their third year, in order to choose how to complete their curriculum with courses that are not compulsory for everyone.
Students can present or ask to renew their educational path only once for each academic year, according to the following time frame.

The window for presenting the educational path is September 15 -- November 30.

HOW AND WHEN IS THE EVALUATION OF THE TRAINING PATHS SUBMITTED CARRIED OUT?
Training courses will be assessed and approved within a few weeks by the competent Commission, so students are advised to wait to write in before the end of the system.
Students are advised to write in after two weeks unless strictly necessary. In the case of non-approval, students will be informed of the changes needed to make the
study plan according to the study plan regulations. A student may resubmit a new course with the
necessary corrections immediately after notification of non-approval.

HOW TO CONTACT THE COMPETENT COMMISSION?
The reference lecturer in the competent commission for the evaluation and approval of training courses for the
degree courses is Prof. Angelo Spognardi, whom you can write to for further information and/or justified requests
for urgent approval (e.g., a learning agreement for the Erasmus programme).
