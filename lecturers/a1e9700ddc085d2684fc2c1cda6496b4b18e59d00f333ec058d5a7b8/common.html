Ivan Visconti got his Laurea (M.Sc. equivalent) cum laude in Computer Science in 1998 (first in his class) and his PhD in Computer Science in 2003 from Università di Salerno. Then he has been a post-doctoral researcher in Paris, at the Département d'Informatique of the École Normale Supérieure, and then assistant/associate/full professor (since 2019) at the University of Salerno.
Since 2024 he is a full professor of computer science at Sapeinza University of Rome.

Ivan Visconti's research focuses on advanced cryptographic notions and tools for data security, privacy, blockchain technology, zero-knowledge proofs, post-quantum cryptography, secure computation, cryptographic protocols, e-voting, privacy-enhancing cryptography, decentralization. He has been a local scientific coordinator for the H2020 European project "PRIViLEDGE" (Privacy-Enhancing Cryptography in Distributed Ledgers), that focused on security Blockchain technology and their applications (2018-2021) and of the MIUR project "PrepAring cRypTograpHy for privacy-awarE blockchaiN applicatiONs (PARTHENON)".

Ivan Visconti received a Certificate of Merit "for outstanding editorial board service for the IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security". In 2022 he got the best paper award for the conference CANS.

Results of his research have been published in the most important worldwide conferences on theoretical computer science (STOC (1), FOCS (2)) and cryptography (CRYPTO 11), EUROCRYPT (6)). He has been several times program committee member of IACR conferences and of ICALP 2015 (track A). See csrankings.org to have an idea about the importance of some conferences in computer science.
