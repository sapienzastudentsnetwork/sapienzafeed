Johannes Brüstle is a Fixed-Term Assistant Professor (RTDA) at Sapienza University of Rome. He completed his PhD in Mathematics at the London School of Economics and previously earned his MSc in Computer Science and BSc in Math and Computer Science from McGill University. His main research interests lie in Algorithmic Game Theory. His work focuses on topics including posted price mechanisms, prophet inequalities (and prediction augmented prophet inequalities), and online optimization. He is also actively working on projects related to the analysis and mechanism design for LLMs, online learning in simple auctions, and information design for first price auctions.
